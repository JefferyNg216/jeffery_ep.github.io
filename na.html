Numerical Analysis

Overall, the key learning outcome of Numerical Analysis can be outline to:-

  • Statistics type, measurement and framework
  • Calculation, chart presentation and analyst methodology by using R studio
  • Key function of statistics (deviation, probability, type of distribution, events)
  • Testing and selection of hypothesis (Parametric and Nonparametric)
  • Correlation and regression analysis
  • The corresponding test to act in R and choose the appropriate function
  • Interpet finding and results (e.g. p-value, CI, r-value)
  • Bayes theorem and Bayesian Data Analysis

    • During the module, there are three major activities carried out. First, the utilization of R to perform basic algorithm, converting variables, graph and plots visualization, such as:-

  • Load the statistics file (dta, csv, sav)
  • Install and apply different type of packages
  • Converting data type to factor and understand its label
  • Employ the summary of variable by using summary() to get the min, max, median, 1st Qu, 3rd Qu and mean
  • Create frequency table by using table()
  • Create graph/plots and design its style by using boxplot(), ggplot(), geom_boxplot() and barplot()
  • Perform different types of correlation test, normality test, hypothesis test, e.g. cor.test(), ad.test(), skewness(), t.test() shapiro.test(), etc…

    • Second, to understand the usage of different tests and its characteristics:-

  • Parametric (Not normally distributed and compare the distribution by mean), e.g. t-Tests & ANOVA
  • Nonparametric (Not normally distributed and compare the distribution by median), e.g. Chi-Square Test & Mann-Whitney U test
  • The null hypothesis of difference test
  • Correlation test (Pearson correlation coefficient by r value)

    • Third, interpreting the result and analyst of different tests, e.g.

  • Descriptive Statistics vs Inferential Statistics
  • Interpret CI, which contain 0 is suggest that there is a statistically significant
  • Interpret p-value, which is less than 0.05, the null hypothesis will be rejected
  • Interpret r-value, which is equal to 0 means there is no correlation between variables, and greater or less than 0.5 / -0.5 means there is strong correlation between variables

    • For those skill, knowledge and methodology learnt during the module, the learning induced to think about how to employing them to my daily living, such that I, as a Data Analyst, when new data pipeline require to development or new business objective require to represent, can the hypothesis test of association and correlation test are one of the way to prove the data relevant and provide more meaningful analysis? Also, when doing some personal analysis like stock trading, are those methods also applicable for further examination?

    In general, the Numerical Analysis module is quite different from the others that I have undergone, it requires a deeper research and self-motivation to understand each theory and method, otherwise, it’s hard to carry on to the next stage.
